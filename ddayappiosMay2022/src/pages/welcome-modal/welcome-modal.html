Welcome To D-Day
Homage

The sculpture Homage is a symbolic piece, emblematic of those communities across the country that nurtured those who went away to war and grieved for those who never returned.

Richard S. Reynolds Sr. Garden

The Richard S. Reynolds, Sr. Garden features a full-figure bronze statue of General Dwight D. Eisenhower and a mosaic tile replica of the battle map used for the invasion located on the ceiling of the English folly.

Memorial Wall

The names of the 2,502 United States service members killed on D-Day are found on the wall on the western side of the plaza. Names of the fallen 1,913 Allies appear on the eastern wall.

Gray Plaza

While visiting Gray Plaza, one finds statuary representative of the beach landing and fighting. This includes two obstacles in the reflecting pool, a landing craft representation, and several figural pieces.

Overlord Arch

The Overlord Arch in Estes Plaza stands forty-four and one-half feet high (representative of the date of D-Day, 6 June 1944). The Plaza celebrates the breaching of Fortress Europe, the foothold in France.

Gold Star Families Memorial Monument

In both World Wars, a family which had lost a loved one in uniform hung a banner with a vivid Gold Star in their window—hence the term Gold Star Families. This monument—Virginia’s first—recognizes the sacrifices of the families left behind when heroes pay the ultimate sacrifice.
